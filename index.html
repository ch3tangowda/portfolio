Deconstructing physics behind Float serve (Thesis Project)

Float serve is a volleyball serve used by professional atheletes where the ball swerves randomly after crossing the net. My Life

Growing up in Kaiga, tucked deep in the Western Ghats, adventure was never a hobby—it was just life. I spent my childhood diving headfirst into reservoir waters, stumbling upon hidden waterfalls with no map in hand, and wasting hours lounging by the riverbanks. It was raw, wild, and completely normal. These days, I’m based in Bangalore, navigating the 9-to-5 grind. But come the weekend, that old itch kicks in. Only now, I chase the same adrenaline with a roaring bike and a full tank—still wild at heart, just with better gear.

When I’m not wandering out of the city on weekends, I usually like to keep things moving. You’ll usually find me rock climbing, out for a run, doing laps in a pool, or smashing birdies on a badminton court. If I’m not in the mood to move, I dive into my little world of hobby projects—tinkering with a Raspberry Pi, plotting model plane builds, or randomly deciding to learn something new, like, say... building this very website (yes, that is how it started). I’ll probably throw together something protein-packed in the kitchen too, just to feel extra productive. But honestly, nothing beats goofing around with my crew.

I’m not a hardcore foodie, but I’m always up to explore new cuisines, dig up hidden gems or just wander and explore Bangalore. Hit me up with recommendations—or even better, would love some great company to share it with.
